## Overview
This research project focuses on analyzing PM2.5 air pollution data using low-cost air quality sensors and environmental datasets. The study investigates pollution trends, sensor bias, wildfire smoke influence, and meteorological impacts on air quality measurements.

The project combines data preprocessing, statistical analysis, visualization, and environmental data interpretation to better understand air pollution behavior and sensor reliability.

---

## Objective
The primary objectives of this research are to:
- Analyze PM2.5 air pollution trends
- Study bias in low-cost air quality sensors
- Investigate the impact of wildfire smoke and meteorological conditions
- Compare sensor measurements across different locations
- Improve understanding of environmental monitoring systems

---

## Research Topics
- PM2.5 trend analysis
- Wildfire smoke contribution to sensor bias
- Meteorological influence on air quality
- Low-cost sensor evaluation
- Environmental data visualization
- Pollution pattern analysis

## Datasets Used
- PurpleAir Sensor Data
- EPA Air Quality Data
- Meteorological Data
- Regional PM2.5 Environmental Measurements

---

## Methodology

### Data Preprocessing
- Cleaned missing and inconsistent records
- Standardized timestamps
- Merged environmental and meteorological datasets
- Filtered invalid sensor readings

### Data Analysis
- Statistical analysis of PM2.5 concentrations
- Correlation analysis between environmental variables
- Time-series trend analysis
- Distribution and variability analysis

### Visualization
Generated visualizations including:
- Time-series plots
- Box plots
- Scatter plots
- Correlation heatmaps
- Comparative city-level graphs

## Key Findings
- PM2.5 sensor readings vary significantly across locations and environmental conditions
- Wildfire smoke contributes to increased sensor bias
- Meteorological factors such as humidity and temperature affect low-cost sensor accuracy
- Strong regional variability exists in pollution measurements
